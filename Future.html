Future Tense

Usage

The future tense is used to denote something that will occur in the future. This is often done using the grammatical verb "coa," which denotes a future tense. However, the use of this verb, while common, is not universal. Some variants of Erdic choose to use the present progressive conjugation instead.

"Coa" Method

To conjugate in this way, the verb "coa" is conjugated as if it were the active verb, and is then proceeded by the present form of a verb.

Coir vida nevo.
Translated: I will live again.

Present Progressive Method

To conjugate using the present progressive, the verb suffix -v is tacked onto the end of the present conjugation of the verb, as shown in the table below:
Subject Conjugation of "to live" Translation
Wa Vidirv I am living/I will live
Wi Vidwav We are living/We will live
Yye Vidiev You are living/You will live
Diaet Vidiaetev You are all living/You will all live
Heam/Heac/Lo Vidurv (He/She/It) is living/(He/She/It) will live
Heamen Videnv They are living/They will live


Vidirv nevo.
Translated: I will live (I am living) again.
