Commands

Informal Commands

A vast majority of verbs terminate in -a or -ij in their infinitive forms. In these cases, the subject of the command is omitted and the -a or -ij suffix is removed. If the verb is monosyllabic and terminates in -a or -ij, then the terminating suffix is replaced with -e.
When using verbs that do not terminate in -a or -ij, it is most common for the entire verb to be left intact, and for the subject to be omitted.

-a polysyllabic verb:
Vid nevo.
Translated: Live again.

-a monosyllabic verb:
Ce ã oze.
Translated: Go home.

other verb:
L'kalb, ebe.
Translated: Suck it, please.

Formal Commands

Formal commands use the same conjugation as the present second person, and the subject does not need to be omitted. In some dialects, it is more polite to state the subject after the verb.

Formal Command using post-predicate honorifics:
Esie gwã, yyesera.
Translated: Eat well, sir.
