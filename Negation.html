Negation

Negation is applied to a concept via the prefix ni- applied to a verb.

Arkan nisur beer.
Translated: Cruelty is not sexy.

N' Contraction

When the negation ni- is prepended onto a word that begins in a vowel sound, it is replaced with the contracted version, n'. In many dialects, this contraction is also applied to words beginning in the /h/ sound. In these cases, the /h/ is not pronounced in the negated form.

Heam n'esur je kor.
Translated: He does not eat the food.

With Use of Object Pronoun Prefixes

When combined with object pronoun prefixes, Erdic negation usually comes first. Some dialects of Erdic allow object pronoun prefixes to come first when the affected verb does not begin with /h/ nor a vowel sound. However, in any dialect, negation, when combined with object pronoun prefixes, never takes the form n', and instead always takes the form of ni-.

(Depending on dialect)
Heam nil'dijur.
Heam l'nidijur.
Translated: He does not say it.
